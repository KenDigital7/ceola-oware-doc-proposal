# Project Brief: Living Family Archive (Father’s Story)

**Source:** Discovery conversation (transcript in `Context/Ceola's Dad's Story.txt`)  
**Prepared for:** Internal use → proposal tailoring  
**Producer:** Ken Williams Jr · Kenctures Inc  

---

## Client & urgency

- **Client:** Ceola — South Side Chicago; deeply motivated after losing her mother with “so many unanswered questions.”
- **Subject:** Her father — described as a living holder of family history knowledge that even older siblings may not have; men in the family line shared more with him. **Time-sensitive:** preserving this thread before it’s gone.
- **Practical constraint:** Father has **gout** and difficulty walking → **in-home production** is preferred.

---

## Core goals

1. **Archive first** — Capture father’s voice and lineage story in a durable, shareable form (not “content for content’s sake”).
2. **Intergenerational bridge** — Ghana ↔ Chicago ↔ global diaspora (cousins abroad, elders passing, younger generation not always present for funerals/rites).
3. **Proof of concept** for a **longer “living” docu-series** — start with father; later expand to **Tribe Association** members and broader cultural/diaspora narratives.
4. **Distribution is open, not required for success** — Open to festivals, public media, Netflix-level interest; **primary win** = family preservation and honest history.

---

## Narrative arc (as described)

| Phase | Content |
|-------|--------|
| **1. Family history (Ghana)** | Who is related to whom; lineage from the elder’s perspective. |
| **2. Chicago / migration** | How he came to Chicago; honoring **cousins who came early** and have since passed — reference points for the family. |
| **3. Extended family threads** | e.g. sister’s story (Paris → Chicago, long presence in city) — “little” legacies that deserve to be on record. |
| **4. Tribe Association & community** | Father **founded / helped lead** the **Tribe Association** (tribal/cultural association in Ghanaian diaspora context); served as **international president** — institutional memory worth documenting. |
| **Future** | Interviews with **Tribe Association** members; other Ghanaian associations (e.g. Ashanti, etc.) — **scalable**, Ghana “trending” as cultural moment to tell real stories of contribution to Chicago and the US |

## Creative / production preferences

- **Location:** **His home** — comfortable, authentic; avoid turning project into a burden.
- **Look:** Manage **clutter** — option: **pull-up / step-and-repeat style backdrop** OR use a **wall with family photos** after light staging; client also likes idea of **B&W family imagery on screen** behind him for “legacy layer.”
- **Archival:** Client takes many **photos** — integrate **stills + documents** where possible to break up talking-head and ground stories in evidence.
- **Music / score:** Needs **licensing clarity** — royalty-free vs. composer; producer can advise/source.
